ABOUT
I build systems where correctness is non-negotiable. My career sits at the intersection of quantitative finance, distributed systems, and formal methods, first on traditional trading desks, then in designing core protocol economics for billion-dollar blockchain networks, and now as the founder of BitFrost Prime.
BitFrost Prime is the first formally verified cross-venue clearing and prime brokerage protocol for crypto derivatives. The architecture is designed using the same class of formal methods used to verify NASA flight software, and AWS distributed systems. The protocol introduces a single clearing primitive to eliminate the cascading failures plaguing current DeFi infrastructure.
Before founding BitFrost, I led protocol research at Lombard (Bitcoin liquid staking, $1B TVL in 60 days). I served as Chief Protocol Economist at Babylon Labs, where I co-authored the economic design that secured a $90M Series A from Paradigm, Polychain, and Bullish. Earlier, I spent five years in quantitative trading building the execution and research stack at Citadel's natural gas desk, running multi-strategy crypto books at Sharpe >1.5, and developing Kalman-filter stat-arb models for G10 FX.
I hold a PhD in Applied Mathematics from MIT. My technical surface area spans Rust/Go/Python systems programming, Cosmos SDK and Solana protocol development, formal specification (Quint/TLA+/Apalache), and machine learning pipelines for trading signals. I think in terms of state machines, invariants, and measure theory, and I ship production code.
